## 1. MANDATORY PRE-FLIGHT — RUN BEFORE EVERY BLOG

Before writing any blog post, complete these checks:

1. **Cannibalisation check** — Search existing posts at zasupport.com/blog for overlapping topics. If >60% topic overlap exists with an existing post, UPDATE that post instead of creating new.
2. **Keyword validation** — Confirm primary keyword has search volume (use web search to verify "People Also Ask" and autocomplete suggestions).
3. **SERP analysis** — Check what currently ranks for the target keyword. Note word count, headings, and content gaps in top 3 results.
4. **Author assignment** — Every post MUST have a named author (courtney-bentley or mary-blount). Never publish without authorship.

## 2. BLOG POST STRUCTURE — EVERY POST MUST INCLUDE

### Required elements (no exceptions):

```
[Breadcrumbs]          Home / Blog / Category / Post Title
[Category tag]         Repairs | How-To | Pricing | Troubleshooting | Enterprise | Advice | Upgrades | Data Recovery
[H1 headline]          Question-format preferred for featured snippets
[Last updated date]    Published DD Month YYYY · Updated DD Month YYYY
[Key Takeaways box]    3-5 bullet points summarising the post (GEO optimisation)
[Table of Contents]    Anchor jump links to all H2 sections
[Body content]         Min 1,500 words for repair guides, min 800 words for troubleshooting
[Original photos]      At least 1 workshop photo per post with descriptive alt text
[FAQ section]          3-6 questions in H3 question format with 40-60 word answers
[Author box]           Photo + name + role + bio + social links + "View all articles" link
[CTA block]            Assessment booking CTA with phone + WhatsApp + /book link
[Related posts]        3 internal links to related blog posts
```

### Required schema (auto-generated by components):

```
BlogPosting          — headline, author (Person), datePublished, dateModified, publisher (Organization)
BreadcrumbList       — Home > Blog > Category > Post Title
FAQPage              — All FAQ Q&As from the post
Organization         — Site-wide (in layout.tsx via SiteSchema component)
Person               — Author entity with sameAs, knowsAbout, worksFor
```

### Optional schema (add when applicable):

```
HowTo                — Repair guide posts with step-by-step instructions
AggregateRating      — Include 4.9/5 from 632 Google Reviews on relevant posts
```

## 4. E-E-A-T CONTENT RULES

### Experience (first E)
- ALWAYS include first-person workshop experience: "In our Hyde Park workshop, we see..."
- Reference specific repair counts, timelines, and outcomes from real cases
- Include original photos from the workshop (not stock images)
- Describe tools used: hot-air rework station, stereo microscope, ultrasonic cleaner
- Reference South African context: load shedding, Johannesburg climate, Rand pricing

### Expertise
- Use correct technical terminology (logic board, not motherboard; trackpad, not touchpad)
- Reference Apple's official documentation and link to support.apple.com where relevant
- Include specific model identifiers (A2338, MacBook Pro 14-inch M1 Pro)
- Cite component numbers and repair procedures accurately

### Authoritativeness
- Link to the /author/courtney-bentley page from every post
- Reference "since 2009" and "25,000+ repairs" as authority markers
- Mention Apple Developer Program membership
- Mention BEE Level 1 certification for SME/enterprise content

### Trustworthiness
- Always display transparent pricing (assessment from R599)
- Include warranty terms (up to 3 years on components)
- Link to the /editorial-policy page from the blog footer
- Display Google Reviews rating (4.9 stars, 632 reviews) via schema
- Include "No Fix No Fee" promise where applicable

## 5. CONTENT VELOCITY RULES

### Safe publishing cadence for zasupport.com:
- **Maximum:** 2-3 posts per day, published at consistent times
- **Minimum:** 3 posts per week to maintain freshness signals
- **NEVER:** Batch-publish more than 5 posts in a single day
- **NEVER:** Go more than 7 days without publishing
- **Consistency over volume:** Same cadence every week beats sporadic bursts

### Topic distribution (per month):
- 40% Repair guides (logic board, liquid damage, screen, battery)
- 20% How-to/troubleshooting (WiFi, slow Mac, black screen, backup)
- 15% Pricing/cost guides (transparent pricing for Johannesburg searches)
- 10% Enterprise/SME (JAMF, MDM, managed services, POPIA)
- 10% Product advice (repair vs replace, upgrade guides)
- 5% Industry news (macOS updates, Apple hardware releases, SA context)

## 6. INTERNAL LINKING RULES

Every blog post MUST include:
1. **3-5 links to other blog posts** (not just service pages)
2. **1-2 links to service pages** (repair hub, booking page)
3. **1 link to the author page**
4. **1 outbound link** to Apple Support or another authoritative source

### Link anchor text:
- Use descriptive anchors: "MacBook liquid damage repair process" not "click here"
- Vary anchor text — never use the same anchor for the same target across posts
- Link from high-traffic posts to newer posts to pass authority

## 8. MONTHLY CONTENT AUDIT

On the 1st of every month:

1. **Check all published posts** for outdated pricing, warranty terms, or model references
2. **Update dateModified** on any post that receives content changes
3. **Review cannibalisation** — identify posts competing for the same keyword and consolidate
4. **Check broken links** — verify all internal and external links are functional
5. **Review Google Search Console** — identify posts ranking positions 5-20 that could be improved
6. **Update FAQ sections** — add new questions from GSC "Queries" data
7. **Add new internal links** — link from older posts to newer relevant content
