# Content Mix Strategy — zasupport.com

**Question:** What percentage of blog posts should be proprietary business experience content, and how do we balance B2B content to drive SMB/enterprise queries without losing the B2C on-ramp?

**Short answer:** **40% proprietary experience minimum across every post** (woven into the content, not as a separate category), and shift the topical mix from the current 35:15:50 (B2C:B2B:local-geographic) toward **45:30:25 (B2C:B2B:cluster-hub)** over 12 weeks. The B2B share doubles. The B2C on-ramp is protected because the absolute count of B2C posts grows even as the percentage drops — the velocity ramp from 8/day to 20/day creates room for both.

**Date:** 21 May 2026
**Method:** Primary-source research + verified zasupport.com sitemap classification + cognitive comprehensive-trigger-protocol + mechanical gate

## 3. The proprietary-experience percentage — answered two ways

The question of "how much proprietary content" has two equally valid framings, and the answer is different for each:

### Framing A — As a content category (separate from how-to / news / pricing)

Recommended split:

| Category | % of all new posts | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Proprietary experience / case studies / original insights** | **20%** | Authority and thought-leadership tier |
| Bottom-of-funnel commercial intent | 25% | Service pages, comparison, "vs Apple Store" |
| Educational top-of-funnel | 35% | How-to, troubleshooting, definitions |
| Local geographic (cluster hubs) | 15% | Suburb-specific repair pages |
| News / industry response | 5% | WWDC, iPhone events, Apple corporate moves |

This is the **per-post classification** view — every new post falls into exactly one bucket.

### Framing B — As a quality dimension (woven through every post)

This is the more important framing.

**Every post should contain at least 40% proprietary experience content by volume, regardless of category.**

The 23% traffic gain for first-hand experience content [BKND, Feb 2026] applies to the *presence* of original insight, not to the *category* of the post. A consumer "MacBook won't turn on" how-to becomes a proprietary-experience post when it contains:

- Specific failure-rate data from the ZA Support workshop ("of 847 MacBooks brought in for this fault in 2025, 62% were charger issues")
- Photos from the workshop showing the actual diagnostic process
- A diagnostic checklist Mary or Courtney uses on intake
- The specific tools used (without naming them — per measurement-instrument-language-ban skill)
- Outcomes data ("we recovered the data in 92% of cases")

Without this layer, the post is generic. With it, the post is uniquely ZA Support.

**Recommendation: 40% by volume per post, plus 20% of posts dedicated entirely to proprietary categories.** Combined floor: roughly half of all blog content surface is content no competitor can replicate.

## 5. The B2B content categories that move the needle

Not all B2B content is equal. Based on MSP-specific research:

### Tier 1 — High-conversion (priority build)

1. **Vertical-specific compliance guides** — "POPIA compliance for medical practices on Mac", "FAIS compliance for financial services Mac fleets", "POPIA for legal practices with Apple devices"

4. **Risk assessment / business case** — "What it costs an accounting firm when a partner's MacBook fails for a day", "Hidden cost of consumer-grade IT support for medical practices"

## 6. The proprietary-experience content patterns that actually work

What "first-hand experience" looks like in practice for each category:

### For B2C how-to posts

| Generic version (penalty risk) | Proprietary version (+23% gain pattern) |
|---|---|
| "Try restarting your MacBook in safe mode" | "Of the last 200 MacBooks that came into our workshop with this fault, 73% resolved with this single step in under 90 seconds. The other 27% needed [specific path]" |
| "Liquid damage is serious" | "We've recovered 184 liquid-damaged MacBooks since 2020. The first 60 minutes matter more than anything you'll do in the next month — here's the exact sequence" |
| "Battery replacement is recommended every 3 years" | "Workshop data from 312 MacBook batteries we've replaced shows 11th gen Intel batteries last 2.4 years average in JHB heat, M-series 3.1 years" |

### For B2B vertical content

| Generic | Proprietary |
|---|---|
| "Medical practices need POPIA compliance" | "We've onboarded 14 medical practices to compliant Mac fleets since POPIA came into force. The three compliance failures we see most often during practice audits are..." |
| "MDM solutions for SMB" | "We've deployed Jamf to 8 SMB fleets, Kandji to 5, and Mosyle to 11. The decision tree we use to recommend one over the others is..." |
| "Backup is important" | "Of 47 ransomware incidents we've responded to in the last 24 months, 31 recovered data in under 4 hours because of backup. 16 lost everything. Here's the backup structure for each of those 31" |

### For case studies (Tier 1 B2B)

Anonymised structure:

1. Client type (law firm / dental practice / accounting firm) without name
2. Fleet size and starting state
3. Specific problem they came to ZA Support with
4. The diagnostic and decision path
5. The implementation
6. Measurable outcomes (uptime, ticket volume, costs)
7. What they would do differently if they could restart

Two case studies per month, every month. Over 12 months that's 24 case studies — a content asset competitors cannot replicate because they don't have the operational history.

## 8. Why 30% is the right B2B ceiling (not 50%)

The temptation is to push B2B harder because it converts at higher value. The reason 30% is the right ceiling, not 50%:

### Reason 1 — Search volume math

Consumer Mac repair queries vastly outnumber B2B IT queries in Johannesburg:

- "macbook repair johannesburg" — high volume
- "mac repair near me" — high volume
- "iphone screen repair" — high volume
- "MDM solution for Johannesburg SMB" — low volume but high commercial intent
- "managed IT services Johannesburg" — moderate volume, very high commercial intent

If B2B is 50% of content, you starve the B2C funnel that drives the call volume the phone-call-conversion model depends on. The 633 reviews exist because of B2C volume.

### Reason 2 — The lead-quality math

A B2B lead is worth 10-50x a B2C repair quote (12-month retainer vs single repair). But B2C lead frequency is 50-100x B2B lead frequency. The expected value is similar at the funnel level. Heavy weighting toward B2B sacrifices certain B2C revenue for uncertain B2B revenue.

### Reason 3 — The brand-positioning math

ZA Support's brand strength is "best Mac repair in Johannesburg" (633 reviews 4.9). The B2B brand "best Apple IT managed services in SA" is being built. Pivoting too hard toward B2B before the B2B brand has been earned risks confusing both audiences.

### Reason 4 — The competitive math

Stuff SA, MyBroadband, ITWeb, BusinessTech cover B2B IT topics. ZA Support is best positioned on Mac-specific B2B IT, not on generic IT. 30% lets ZA Support own the Mac-specific B2B niche without trying to compete on the saturated generic IT niche.
