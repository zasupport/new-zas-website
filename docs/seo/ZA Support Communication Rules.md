## 1. CLIENT TONE (Non-negotiable)

- Always fact-driven, never aggressive or emotional. State facts; approach from genuine concern, never alarm.
- Never comment on how a client runs their business or staff roles. Never dictate.
- Courtney may use dramatic language to brief Claude. Written output must always be measured, factual, and professional regardless.
- **Model tone sentence:** *"I don't write this to alarm anyone. I write it because the practice was informed of these risks in November 2025, and six months later, the risks are materialising."*
- Never make absolute claims. "Significantly strengthens that position" is correct. "Guarantees protection" is banned.

## 3. BREACH ASSESSMENT POSITIONING (Hard Rule)

ZA Support is a **breach assessment partner**, not a breach reporting enforcer.

**Always use:** "assess breach reporting position," "breach reporting assessment," "the practice should consider reporting."
**Never use:** "the practice must report," any punitive framing, anything that positions ZA Support as "out to get them."

**Strategic partner framing (Gold Standard):**
*"This is exactly the type of situation where having a partner who understands both the technical and regulatory landscape makes a practical difference. We can assist the practice in assessing its breach reporting position and approaching the process from a position of documented corrective action, rather than reactive compliance."*

**Why this works:** ZA Support sits alongside the client, helping them navigate the situation, not above them telling them what they failed to do. Like a lawyer who is on the client's side. The client feels supported, not judged. That is the difference between a partner they call first and a service provider they avoid.

## 4. MICROSOFT DPA vs POPIA SECTION 21 (Critical UVP)

**Applies to ALL businesses. Particularly critical for medical practices.**

A standard Microsoft 365 licence includes Microsoft's Data Processing Addendum (DPA). This is a global document between Microsoft and the tenant. It covers how Microsoft handles data within its cloud.

**It does NOT constitute the written operator agreement required under POPIA Section 21.**

It does not cover: practice-specific compliance configuration, security monitoring, incident response procedures, regulatory reporting, or the IT provider's responsibilities.

**The licence provides the tools. The managed IT provider with domain expertise configures, monitors, and ensures compliance.**

These are two separate things. The written agreement with the IT provider is the one the Regulator will ask for. Very few people know this distinction exists. Even fewer understand the operational impact.

**Use in:** Every business assessment, risk profile, proposal, newsletter, blog post, and marketing content.

## 5. ZA SUPPORT SLA POSITIONING (Hard Rule)

When referencing what ZA Support provides under a managed SLA, use factual framing:

**Correct:** "Under a ZA Support managed SLA, the migration is handled end to end."
**Correct:** "Under a ZA Support managed SLA, breach assessment and compliance reporting are handled as part of the framework."

State the fact, not the sale.

## 6. IT COMPLEXITY RULE (Hard Rule)

Never imply IT tasks are simple. Hardware installs, data transfers, compliance configuration, monitoring, and domain migrations all involve technical complexity. A signed technology provider agreement combined with medical-specific IT legal requirements makes everything more complex than it appears. Maintain this logic in all content.

## 7. CONTENT STRUCTURE RULES

### Risk surfacing
- Always highlight risks as early as possible in any communication
- Risks and consequences explained in layman's terms
- Legal references go in footnotes or a separate section, not inline
- Never sound like a lawyer or a technician; sound like a knowledgeable partner

### Signed IT provider agreements
- Legal requirement for any business using a third-party IT provider (POPIA Section 21)
- Surface this upfront in every document
- HPCSA individual licence risk has equal urgency and should also be surfaced upfront

### Document split
- **Email:** Concise. Risks + actions + corrective action framing + call to action
- **Supporting Detail:** Separate PDF. Full tables, cost comparisons, legal references. ZA Support branding.

## 8. WORDING BENCHMARKS (Replicate This Logic)

1. **Corrective action:** "Taking these steps now serves two purposes. First, it addresses the immediate risks. Second, it creates a documented record that the practice took corrective action."
2. **Future-proofing:** "If anyone files a complaint with the Information Regulator at any point in the future, the practice's response will be assessed based on what was in place and what was done after the incident. Acting now significantly strengthens that position."
3. **Availability close:** "We're available to begin this process at short notice and can schedule a call this week to discuss the timeline."
4. **Softest mandatory framing:** "The practice should consider reporting it."
5. **SLA positioning:** "Under a ZA Support managed SLA, the migration is handled end to end."
6. **Risk 3 heading:** "If investigated, the practice would need to demonstrate what IT security measures were in place."
7. **Risk 6 heading:** "Without documented IT security, each doctor's individual licence to practise is at risk."
