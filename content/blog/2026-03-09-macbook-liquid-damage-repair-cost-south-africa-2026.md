## Why Liquid Damage Is More Complicated Than a Cracked Screen

A cracked screen is a physical problem with a predictable solution. Liquid damage is an entirely different category of repair. When liquid enters a MacBook, it doesn't stay in one place. Capillary action draws moisture across the logic board, under chips, into connectors, and between layers of the PCB before you even have time to unplug the power cable.

The real damage isn't actually the liquid itself — it's the **oxidation and corrosion** that begins within hours of contact, especially when the machine is powered on while wet. The minerals and salts left behind by water, coffee, juice, or wine act as conductors, creating short circuits across components that were never meant to be connected.

This is why the speed of your response matters enormously, and why understanding the repair process — and the associated costs — helps you make an informed decision quickly.

### What Determines Where Your Repair Falls in That Price Range?

Every liquid damage case is unique. Several factors influence the final cost of your water damage MacBook repair in Johannesburg:

**1. The Type of Liquid**
Water is the least damaging liquid — it contains fewer minerals and evaporates cleanly. Coffee, tea, juice, soft drinks, and wine are far more corrosive because they contain sugars, acids, and mineral salts that accelerate oxidation. Saltwater (from coastal humidity or ocean spray) is among the worst.

**2. How Long the Machine Was Powered On After the Spill**
If you immediately powered off and left the machine off, you've given yourself a much better outcome. Every second a wet logic board remains energised, electrical shorts are causing additional component damage. Machines brought in within 24–48 hours of the incident consistently have better outcomes and lower repair costs.

**3. The Extent of Corrosion on the Logic Board**
Our technicians assess the board under microscopes. Light surface oxidation on connector pads is a relatively straightforward clean. Corrosion that has penetrated under BGA chips, damaged power management circuits, or eaten through PCB traces requires significantly more microsoldering work — and that is reflected in the cost.

**4. Component Failures**
Sometimes the liquid causes specific components to fail: the keyboard, trackpad, battery, I/O board, or display backlight circuit. Each failed component adds to the overall repair cost. We always identify these during the R599 assessment so there are no surprises.

**5. MacBook Model and Generation**
Newer M-series MacBooks use more tightly integrated silicon, which can make certain microsoldering tasks more complex. Older Intel models have more traditional logic board layouts. Both are repairable — the model simply affects the technical labour involved.

## What You Should Do Right Now If Your MacBook Got Wet

If the spill just happened — or happened recently — here are the immediate steps that maximise your chances of a full recovery:

1. **Power off immediately.** Hold the power button until the screen goes black. Do not just close the lid.
2. **Do not press any keys or try to restart it.** Every keystroke on a wet keyboard risks sending liquid further into the machine.
3. **Disconnect all cables and peripherals,** including the MagSafe or USB-C charger.
4. **Turn the MacBook upside down in a tent shape** to encourage any pooled liquid to drain downward away from the logic board.
5. **Do not put it in rice.** Rice is ineffective, and it wastes critical time. The longer corrosion is allowed to develop, the more expensive the repair becomes.
6. **Bring it to us as soon as possible.** Same-day assessment is available at our Hyde Park location. [Book your R599 assessment online](/contact) or call us directly.
